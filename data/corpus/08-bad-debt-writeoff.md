# Bad Debt Provisioning and Write-off

## What provisioning is for

A bad debt provision is an accounting estimate of the portion of our receivables that we are unlikely to collect. It is recognised under IFRS 9 as an expected credit loss (ECL) and reduces the net receivables figure on the balance sheet.

Provisioning is not the same as writing off. A provision is an estimate; a write-off is a final accounting entry that removes the receivable from the ledger.

## How the provision is calculated

We use a two-layer model:

1. **Specific provision** — for individually significant or clearly impaired receivables. Applied case-by-case when we know a customer is in trouble (bankruptcy filed, payment plan broken, legal case opened).
2. **General provision** — a matrix applied to the remaining portfolio based on aging bucket and customer risk category.

The matrix (illustrative — the actual rates are reviewed annually):

| Aging bucket | A-risk | B-risk | C-risk | D-risk |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Not yet due | 0.1% | 0.3% | 1.0% | 3.0% |
| 1 to 30 days past due | 0.5% | 1.0% | 3.0% | 8.0% |
| 31 to 60 days past due | 2.0% | 4.0% | 10.0% | 25.0% |
| 61 to 90 days past due | 5.0% | 12.0% | 30.0% | 50.0% |
| Over 90 days past due | 15.0% | 35.0% | 70.0% | 90.0% |

The rates are calibrated against the group's historical loss experience over the last five years and adjusted for forward-looking economic indicators as required by IFRS 9.

## What triggers a specific provision

- Customer files for insolvency, administration, or equivalent.
- A payment plan is broken for a second time.
- A dispute resolution concludes that a portion is uncollectible.
- A customer account has not moved in more than 180 days despite active collections.
- Legal counsel advises that recovery is unlikely.

A specific provision is proposed by the credit analyst, approved by the Credit Manager, and posted by Controlling. The posting is journal-reviewed monthly.

## Write-off

A write-off removes the receivable from the ledger. It is the final accounting step after the recovery process has ended. Triggers:

- Legal recovery has formally closed with no further action available.
- An insolvency administrator has issued a final distribution or a certificate of non-recovery.
- Management decides the cost of continuing to pursue the debt exceeds any plausible recovery.

Approval matrix:

| Write-off amount | Approver |
| --- | --- |
| Up to 5,000 | Credit Manager |
| 5,001 to 50,000 | Credit Director |
| 50,001 to 250,000 | CFO |
| Above 250,000 | CFO + Audit Committee |

## Small-balance write-offs

Small residual balances from rounding, bank fees, or currency differences can be written off without full approval within limits:

- Up to 50 per item, 500 per customer per month.
- Reported monthly by reason code.
- Audited quarterly for patterns.

## Recoveries after write-off

If a written-off debt is recovered later, the cash is posted directly to the income statement as "recovery of bad debt" and does not reinstate the original receivable. The customer master note records the recovery and the collector's incentive plan credits it against targets.

## Governance

The bad debt provision is reported monthly to the group controller and quarterly to the audit committee. Any material movement (month-on-month change above 10%) is explained with a written commentary. The provision rates themselves are recalibrated annually with the external auditors.
