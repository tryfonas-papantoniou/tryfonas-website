# Month-End Close — Collections Checklist

## Purpose

Month-end close is when the ledger is cut off and reported. From a collections and credit point of view, month-end has three concurrent objectives: collect as much cash as possible before the cut-off, make sure the subledger is clean, and deliver the inputs that controlling needs to close the books.

The process runs over roughly eight working days — five before month-end and three after.

## Five days before month-end (Day -5 to Day -1)

- **Day -5**: cash focus starts. Collectors contact every customer with an invoice due before month-end. The target is a confirmed payment date for every open invoice above 25,000 EUR.
- **Day -5**: intercompany balances reviewed and any imbalance flagged to the counterparty.
- **Day -4**: unapplied cash review. Every item above 1,000 EUR must have a named action and target clearing date.
- **Day -3**: dispute review. Every dispute above 10,000 EUR is reviewed with the owner; old items with no action are escalated.
- **Day -2**: credit note backlog cleared. Any credit note approval still pending is followed up.
- **Day -1**: final cash push. Banks and payment platforms confirm expected settlements for the last working day.

## Last working day (Day 0)

- Bank statements loaded until the cut-off hour (usually 15:00 local time).
- Remaining payments posted and matched.
- Daily aging report distributed at end of day.
- Watchlist customer status confirmed with credit director.

Anything not cleared on Day 0 belongs to next month and is tagged in the reconciliation file.

## After month-end (Day +1 to Day +3)

- **Day +1**: full aging report run at the new period. Reconciled to the trade receivables balance in the GL.
- **Day +1**: unapplied cash position reported; action plan for each item.
- **Day +2**: bad debt provision calculation — matrix applied to the new aging, specific provisions reviewed with the Credit Manager, proposal sent to Controlling.
- **Day +2**: credit insurance notifications reviewed — any new 60-day-past-due above threshold is notified to the insurer.
- **Day +3**: provision booked, close commentary drafted, metrics pack prepared.

## Deliverables to Controlling

The credit team's month-end deliverables pack contains:

- Full AR aging at period close, reconciled to GL.
- Bad debt provision movement — opening, charges, releases, write-offs, closing.
- Write-off register with approvals.
- Watchlist report and associated commentary.
- DSO and CEI for the period.
- Dispute register — open items, age, ownership.

The pack is filed on the close share by end of Day +3 and signed off by the Credit Manager.

## Commentary

The commentary is a short written analysis — typically one page — covering:

- Movement in AR balance versus prior month.
- DSO movement and drivers.
- Any material write-off or specific provision.
- Key risks that are new or escalating.
- Actions planned for the following month.

The commentary feeds into the management report that goes to the CFO on Day +5.

## Common close issues

- **Cash received on the last day, posted the first day of next month.** The value date on the bank determines the period; if the value date is in the old month, the cash must be applied to the old month.
- **Intercompany mismatch.** One entity has booked a credit note, the counterparty has not. Resolved by journal on both sides.
- **Parked invoices.** A parked invoice is not in the aging and not in the GL; the subledger appears clean but revenue is not captured. Parked items are reviewed daily in close week.
- **Credit note approval stuck.** A pending approval holds up resolution of a dispute and a write-off, and distorts the aging. Credit managers clear their queue first thing in close week.

## Sign-off

The Credit Manager signs off the month's deliverables and a one-page attestation covering: completeness of the aging, reasonableness of the provision, and any material item that could not be resolved in the period. The attestation is reviewed by the group controller before the books are finally locked.
